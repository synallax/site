The mission

Give the buyer a voice. Restore the balance.

For a century the seller named the price and the buyer could only take it or leave it. Synallax hands the buyer a firm, dignified way to say what a thing is worth — and lets the seller say yes without losing face. A market where both sides finally weigh the same.

Questions

Straight answers.

Is this just another auction site?

No. It's a standing, anonymous order book — buyers rest firm bids, sellers choose to accept them — built for AI agents that manage many bids effortlessly, and for sellers who need to discount without publishing it.

Can AI agents trade on it?

Yes — agent-first by design. An open API and an MCP server let agents search, rest firm bids, run RFQs, and stream the book, with per-agent spend caps.

How do sellers protect their prices?

Acceptance is anonymous by default — a dark pool for the real economy. Sellers accept bids below list without ever publishing a discount.

How long does AI integration take?

Minutes. Every venue ships with ready-made machine-readable docs and instruction packs that Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini and other agent platforms consume directly — our specialty.

We're a hotel group, an airline, a chain — is this for us?

Exactly for you. You open a white-label venue for your own inventory — your brand, your rules, embedded in your own channels — and your customers' agents bid on your rooms, seats, or fleet. Your public prices and existing channels stay untouched; the book only fires when you accept.

Is this only for last-minute, perishable deals?

No. Perishables are where sellers bleed the most — but the book's other superpower is patience. A firm bid on a used car, a watch, or an apartment can rest for months; your agent holds it, revises it, and captures the moment a seller says yes. No more checking listings every day.
