The mission

Give the buyer a voice. Restore the balance.

For a century the seller named the price and the buyer could only take it or leave it. Synallax hands the buyer a firm, dignified way to say what a thing is worth — and lets the seller say yes without losing face. A market where both sides finally weigh the same.

Why now

Bidding died for humans. Agents bring it back.

Naming your own price was stressful, so it faded. An AI agent manages fifty resting bids without blinking. The new commerce rails standardize checkout — but nobody owns the venue where agents actually negotiate. Synallax is that layer.

Fixed prices are a UI limitation, not an economic law.

Questions

Straight answers.

Is this just another auction site?

No. It's a continuous, anonymous order book — buyers rest firm bids, sellers hit them — built for AI agents that manage many bids effortlessly, and for sellers who need to discount without publishing it.

Is Synallax only for travel?

No. It's a generic engine. Flights, hotels, used cars, shared rides, freight — describe any product once and open a venue. Switch the market in the demo above to see it.

Can AI agents trade on it?

Yes — agent-first by design. An open API and an MCP server let agents search, rest firm bids, run RFQs, and stream the book, with per-agent spend caps.

How do sellers protect their prices?

Matching is anonymous by default — a dark pool for the real economy. Sellers accept bids below list without ever publishing a discount.
